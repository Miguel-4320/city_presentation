History

Explore the personalities and historical curiosities that have shaped Lanzarote, especially Arrecife.

Personalities

César Manrique (1919–1992)

Born in Arrecife. An artist, architect and cultural activist, widely known for his influence on Lanzarote's aesthetics.

Jesús del Carmen Soto

A landscape architect and collaborator of Manrique, who introduced aesthetic approaches integrating the volcanic nature.

José Molina Orosa

A doctor from Arrecife who practiced on the island for many decades, making important contributions to public health.

Estanislao González Ferrer

A botanist from Lanzarote, author of important studies on local flora, including several endemic species of the island.

Yonathan de León Machín

Current mayor of Arrecife (since July 2023), noted for being the youngest mayor in the city's history.

Hecher Sosa

Hecher Sosa, known as the "Guanche Warrior," is a Canary Islands–born MMA fighter.

Manolo 'el del bombo'

A well-known popular character, part of the local festive and cultural tradition.

Note: Information about these personalities comes from Wikipedia.

Historical curiosities

Castillo de San Gabriel

Built on "Islote del Quemado" because it was set on fire during an attack by Turkish-Algerian pirates in 1586.

Puente de las Bolas

The access bridge to the castle, famous for its two stone balls, has become a symbol of the historic town.

Orientation of churches

Ancient churches in Lanzarote are often oriented north-northeastward due to wind conditions and terrain, rather than the usual eastward.
